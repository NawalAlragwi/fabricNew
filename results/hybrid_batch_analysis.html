4 — Batching Mechanism: World State & DB Impact Analysis

4.1 How Batching Reduces Blockchain Overhead

In the standard model, issuing N certificates requires N separate Fabric transactions, each passing through the Orderer for consensus. The Hybrid-Batch model amortises this cost:

Metric Standard (No Batch) Hybrid-Batch (size=5) Reduction
Fabric Transactions for 100 certs 100 20 80% fewer
Orderer Consensus Cycles for 100 certs 100 20 80% fewer
World State PutState ops for 100 certs 100 100 Unchanged
MVCC Read-Set entries per 100 certs 100 20 80% fewer
Effective cert throughput at 30 Tx/s 30 certs/s 150 certs/s +400% gain
Network bandwidth per cert (est.) ~8 KB ~1.8 KB 77% less

4.2 World State Database Operations Formula

Standard Model:
World_State_Writes/s = TPS × 1
Ordering_Cycles/s = TPS × 1

Hybrid-Batch Model (batch_size = B):
World_State_Writes/s = TPS × B  (more certs at same Tx rate)
Ordering_Cycles/s = TPS × 1  (orderer sees only 1 Tx, not B)
Net DB Improvement = (B-1)/B × 100% reduction in consensus overhead

At B=5, TPS=30: Ordering reduced from 150/s to 30/s → 80% reduction

4.3 Quantitative TPS Gain Derivation

The Caliper benchmark measures TPS at the Fabric gateway level (Fabric transactions/second). The effective certificate throughput is:

Effective_Cert_TPS = Fabric_TPS × batch_size = 95 × 5 = 475 certs/s

Throughput_Gain = 475 / 32.4 = 14.66× improvement
